# Sam the Side-Project Seeker — Persona 02

> Priority: Primary
> Business goals served: G1 / G2 / G3

---

## Who Sam Is

Sam is 34, a senior agentic engineer at an enterprise. Real AI orchestration is his day job — and he's good at it. Respected, well-compensated, ambitious *not* bitter. The day job is genuinely fine. He has a partner, a real life, and evenings that aren't infinite. He hasn't built the side project he wants because the only side projects that fit his life are *small*, and small is hard to take seriously in a world that valorizes the unicorn pitch.

---

## Psychological Profile

The day job is **quiet success, not escape**. Sam isn't running away from anything; he's running toward a feeling he's never had — what it's like when the AI infrastructure he builds is making money for *him*, not just for the enterprise. He values **his time**, **his life outside work**, and **his craft** — in that order. He takes pride in efficient moves: "I picked the best move in 90 seconds" reads as *self-image*, not just a session pattern.

He is **time-constrained, not capability-constrained**. The blocker isn't ideas or skills — it's the activation cost of starting a "real" side project against finite evenings. spaghetti is not a rehearsal for the real thing and not training wheels. It **is the genuine side project, scaled to a real adult life**. That reframing is load-bearing: the 4-hrs/wk envelope isn't a limit for Sam — it's the *promise*.

Sam is **the most sophisticated possible audience** for the real-AI-ops surface and the **highest-fidelity audit** for the anti-addiction stance. He builds AI systems professionally and has read the design literature on dark patterns. He will detect both authentic domain texture and fake-flavored shovelware within minutes. His trust is hard to win and easy to lose.

---

## Internal State

When Sam opens spaghetti at 9pm on the couch, his partner watching something else, he feels **decompression** layered with **quiet curiosity** — *what did my automation do overnight / today?* — and a flicker of **pride** when the 90-second move is sharp. He's not escaping. He's *checking on his thing*, in the same posture a shopkeeper checks the register before closing up. Underneath: a steady, low **anticipation** for tomorrow morning's overnight reveal, and a constant background **vigilance** for any signal that the game's trust contract is breaking.

---

## Usage Context

**How he finds the product:** Hacker News front page (the Open Data Layer is HN catnip), peer mention in an agentic-engineering Slack/Discord, Tom-Francis-adjacent indie-dev writing about the anti-addiction stance, or the maker's own posts. Sam silently audits the maker before installing.
**Emotional state on arrival:** **Skeptical but curious.** Defenses are up from prior AI-flavored shovelware. Actively scanning the first 90 seconds for tells — real terminology, no FOMO countdown, no signup wall, anti-addiction stance visible in design not just paragraphs, data layer documentation that looks real. If the first 90 seconds pass, the skepticism converts to *"oh, this might actually be the thing."*
**Behavior pattern:** Settles into the Brief's four-cadence pattern, *measured*: 90-sec check-ins (1–3× weekdays, between meetings or refilling coffee); 5–10 min morning ritual tuning the day's automation; weekend 90-min mastermind session (Saturday morning, partner asleep or out); overnight return as 30–60 sec of pure consumption — first thing on phone in bed, or first laptop open of the day. He does *not* open the game on lunch, during meetings, or in response to notifications (there are none). His usage is driven by *his* rhythm, not the game's.
**Decision criteria:** Keeps playing iff (a) the walk-away moment lands within sessions 2–3, (b) zero dark-pattern triggers in the first 5 sessions, (c) the data layer passes the export-and-point-Claude-at-it test in weeks 2–4, (d) real-AI-ops terminology stays consistent and right cumulatively, (e) the economy actually produces the published diminishing-returns curves (he might spot-check, casually). Failure on any of these is **silent churn** — he just stops playing and doesn't tell anyone why.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **The Walk-Away Loop (Goal 1):** Sam *is* the walk-away player by life-fit — real-life constraints (partner, 34, real job) make him the natural 4-hrs/wk operator; his "I picked the best move in 90 seconds" self-image makes the *feeling* land instantly. His telemetry alone validates Obj 1.1 and Obj 1.2.
- ✅ **The Honest Economy (Goal 2):** Sam is the audit. Most equipped to spot-check whether the published diminishing-returns curves emerge from the economy's actual math; most likely to quietly do it once and then trust or churn. His silent-churn behavior makes him the single most informative source on Obj 2.1.
- ✅ **The Open Data Layer (Goal 3):** Sam is the canonical primary user of the data layer and the persona most likely to produce the graduation signal the Brief calls a "slam-dunk." His workflow *is* Obj 3.1's end-to-end test. His peer network is exactly where Obj 3.2's verified-public-instances will come from. His churn-or-not at the week 2–4 first-analysis moment is the make-or-break signal for the entire moat.
